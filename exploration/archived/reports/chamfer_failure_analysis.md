# Stage 3 Chamfer Matching Localization Failure Analysis

> [!NOTE]
> This is a diagnostic audit of the candidate proposals generated by the Stage 3 Chamfer matching engine.
> No system architectures, template sets, or matching parameters were altered during this audit.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This report performs a forensic audit of the **264,852 raw candidate proposals** generated by the Chamfer matching engine across 10 Single Line Diagrams (SLDs). By analyzing the top 100 ranked proposals, we evaluated the localization engine's precision, examined the score distributions, and tracked down the true locations of the MR symbols in all 10 diagrams.

### Key Findings
1. **Zero Precision in Top 100**: There are **zero (0) true MR symbols** in the top 100 ranked proposals. The precision at the top of the ranked list is **0.0%**.
2. **Small Scale & Degenerate Matching Bias**: The top 100 ranked candidates are dominated by scale $0.150$ and $0.178$ proposals (90% of the top 100). Because these templates are extremely small, they have a low number of edge pixels, which easily match straight lines (conductors) or text strokes, yielding very low Chamfer scores (0.48 - 0.58 pixels).
3. **True Symbols are Present but Poorly Ranked**: The actual MR symbols are **not missing entirely** (they are present in the candidate pool for all 10 SLDs), but they are **poorly ranked** (typically between rank 16,000 and 31,000 globally) due to a major scale-mismatch and small-template bias.

### Rationale for Poor Ranking
The primary driver of the poor ranking is the **average distance calculation** in Chamfer matching. 
- **Scale-Mismatch Penalty**: When a large template (e.g. scale 0.35 - 0.45) is matched against the true symbol, it contains 800 - 1000 edge pixels. Minor preprocessing distortions, noise, or template anti-aliasing errors result in non-zero distances for many edge pixels. These errors aggregate, leading to a much higher average Chamfer score (mean distance ~ 6.5 px).
- **Small-Scale Edge Count Advantage**: Small templates (scale 0.15 - 0.178) contain very few edge pixels (100 - 200). They can easily fit completely on straight line segments, elbows, or text label strokes. Because the entire template fits on these segments, every edge pixel has a distance of almost 0, leading to a extremely low Chamfer score (mean distance ~ 0.5 - 0.7 px).
- **Plateau and Basin Attractors**: Small scale models produce a vast number of local minima basins along the long grid lines and character structures of the diagram, which swamp the top of the globally ranked list.

## 6. Recommendations for Stage 4 & 5

Since we cannot alter the architecture or parameters of Stage 3, the following strategies should be implemented in **Stage 4 (Coverage Filtering)** and **Stage 5 (Verification)**:
1. **Edge Coverage Filtering (Stage 4)**: Implement a coverage filter that measures the percentage of template edge pixels that fall within a 1-pixel tolerance of the diagram's edge pixels. For a small template matching a straight line, the coverage might be high, but for a large template matching the actual symbol, the coverage will also be high. 
2. **Template Edge Regularization**: Ensure that templates do not suffer from topological collapse, particularly at smaller scales, which was addressed in Stage 2.7.
3. **Scale-Dependent Score Normalization**: In future stages, we can normalize or weigh the Chamfer score based on the template scale to counteract the small-scale bias.
